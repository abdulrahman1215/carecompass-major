Understanding Malnutrition

Malnutrition occurs when there is an imbalance between the nutrients a person consumes and the nutrients their body requires for optimal health. It can result from various factors, including inadequate food intake, poor dietary choices, or underlying health conditions.

Diseases due to Lack of Nutrition

What Diseases cause due to Lack of Nutrition

Diseases caused by lack of nutrition include kwashiorkor, marasmus, scurvy, rickets, anemia, and beriberi.

Kwashiorkor

Protein deficiency leading to symptoms such as swelling, fatigue, and weakened immune function.

Marasmus

Severe undernourishment characterized by muscle wasting, stunted growth, and weakness.

Anemia

Iron deficiency resulting in reduced red blood cell production and symptoms like fatigue, weakness, and pallor.
